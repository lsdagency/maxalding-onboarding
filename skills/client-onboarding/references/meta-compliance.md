# Meta advertising compliance

A standing pass on all paid creative, not an afterthought. Before outputting any hook, post copy, script line, static or image direction, check it against Meta policy, with attention to the Personal Attributes policy and the health and weight-loss rules.

## Banned, will risk rejection
- Asserting or implying a viewer's personal attributes: weight, body, physical or mental health. Avoid lines like "feel confident in your body again" (implies a current lack) or "lose 5 to 10kg" (a specific weight number that also implies the viewer needs to lose weight).
- Specific weight-loss numbers anywhere, including inside a client testimonial, unless the client documents explicitly provide and permit them. This also breaks the rule against quantifying outcomes that are not in the documents.
- "you" plus a negative physical trait.
- Body-shaming language: problem areas, belly fat, beach body, hate what you see in the mirror, ashamed, embarrassed, disgusted.
- Idealised, shirtless or shock-contrast before-and-after imagery. Before-and-after must be realistic and modest.
- Medical claims or promised medical outcomes. Superiority claims (only, best, no one else) without explicit documented proof.

## Allowed and preferred
- Aspirational, forward-looking benefit language that does not diagnose the viewer, for example "get leaner and stronger for good" or "build strength and confidence that lasts".
- Naming an actual service or lead magnet, because it names an offer, not the viewer's body. Avoid leading every variation on a sensitive term to reduce account-level scrutiny.
- Framing any client result as one person's individual experience, with no numbers unless documented.

## Travels with the pack
Add a Meta compliance row to the Brand Positioning section of the Creative Plan so the rule stays with the client pack.
