# VSL Script guidelines

Document 2, built with build/vsl_script.py. Apply funnel-stage thinking (a paid-traffic asset). Same typography and header as Document 1, no date, logo at top.

## Format
Horizontal 16:9. This is a landing-page video, not a social ad. Do not instruct vertical filming. Recording best practice adapted for landscape: camera on a tripod, frame wide enough for shoulders and above.

## Five-step formula, in order
1. Hook, what it is (about 0:00 to 0:10): open with a direct hook that calls out the audience and their main pain in one breath, then say what this is. For example, "If you're a man in Point Cook or Mornington who wants to get leaner but your training keeps slipping when work takes over, here's your fix." No warm-up, no "hi my name is". Written in the first person, the coach speaking to camera ("I", not "the coach").
2. Why they need it (about 0:10 to 0:35): the problem in the ICP's own daily language. Empathetic, not dramatic. The viewer should think "that is exactly me".
3. What they get (about 0:35 to 1:00): sell the FRONT-END offer, not the full program. If the funnel sells a trial, a free first session or a consultation, describe what they get in THAT session specifically and what they walk out with (for example a plan mapped to their week, a clear first move), framed as outcome and feeling. Remember the VSL is selling the trial sign-up, not the whole coaching program. Do not state price.
4. Why to trust you (about 1:00 to 1:25): first person. Say who you are and, specifically, how you help and transform this exact audience (men in their 30s, busy professionals, whoever the ICP is), then one real client result. Give them enough of you to trust the person on the other side of the offer.

5. Next step (about 1:25 to 1:45): one clear instruction to act now. For a high-ticket funnel, position the private consultation as genuinely valuable (premium framing). For a named free-offer funnel, tell them to register their details in the form below the video to claim it, and name any genuine scarcity (a limited number of free sessions each month), never an invented time deadline. Explain what happens next. End warmly.

## Rules
Written in the first person throughout: the coach speaking as "I", never described in the third person as "the coach" or by name. Full natural sentences, no two-word lines or fragments. Transformation focus throughout. No price. No jargon. No Meta, ads, funnel or marketing terminology. Direction note in italics then the spoken line in grey quotes, with the same timestamp format as Document 1.
