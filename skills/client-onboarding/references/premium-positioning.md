# Premium positioning of the lead magnet

The lead magnet is the way in, but it must never read as cheap or discounted. For a high-ticket offer (for example packages from roughly 300 to 800 dollars), the audience wants to feel they are getting something premium.

- Keep the lead magnet (for example a Fat Loss and Lifestyle Audit) as the entry point.
- Never communicate it with "free", "no cost", "no pressure", "costs nothing" or similar discount language in audience-facing copy.
- Frame it as a private, one-to-one consultation with the coach, where the client leaves with a personalised plan, whether or not they go on to work together.
- Premium vocabulary: private, personalised, complimentary (used sparingly), consultation, expert, tailored.
- Use one consistent premium CTA across a document, for example "Book My Consultation".
